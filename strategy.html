Jottly strategy

Jottly rewards patient deduction over lucky guesses. Here are the habits that turn it into a solving game.

First moves

Open wide.

Your first few words are for gathering letters, not for winning. Play real words packed with different, common letters: the vowels, plus workhorses like R, S, T, N, and L. A good habit is to cover around twenty different letters across your first four guesses. Every letter you spend is one the board can tell you about.

Try AROSE, then UNLIT. Between them that's ten different letters, almost all common, and two scores to point you toward the rest.

One rule that quietly matters: don't repeat a letter inside a single guess. A repeat still scores just one jot, so a word like SPEED only ever tests four distinct letters. Make all five count.

Reading the score

Every number is a clue.

A jot is one of your letters living somewhere in their word. Position never matters, so the number is pure letter overlap. Two ends of the scale are worth the most:

  • A zero is a gift. It rules out all five of your letters at once. That's the most a single move can tell you, so welcome it instead of groaning.
  • A high number early means you're swimming in their letters. Slow down and start isolating rather than guessing whole words.

Jottly marks the obvious in-and-out letters on your keyboard as you go, but the deductions that win games are the ones you make on top of it.

The control trick

Isolate one letter at a time.

Once you've ruled some letters out, those dead letters become tools. Build a word around a single unknown and pad the rest with letters you know are out. Now the only thing that can move your score is the letter you're testing.

Say you've already ruled out B, L, N, and D. Playing BLAND tests one thing: whether their word has an A. The four dead letters can't change the number, so a jot of 1 means yes and a 0 means no. That's elimination run on purpose instead of by luck.

The endgame

Narrow, then anagram.

When a guess scores five jots without solving, you've found all five letters. They're just in the wrong order, and that's the home stretch, not a dead end.

In Jottly you can drag your confirmed letters into any order along the confirmed row. Try the arrangements that actually spell words: lead with common pairings like TH, CH, and ST, keep a vowel near the middle, and you'll usually land it in a move or two.

On defense

Choosing your secret.

A good secret word is fair but not free. Pick a real five-letter word with five different letters (Jottly asks for distinct letters anyway). Lean a little off the beaten path: a word like FJORD or NYMPH hands out fewer early jots than HOUSE does, because its letters are rarer.

Keep it a word a friend could actually find, though. The goal is a satisfying chase, not a word nobody can crack.

Practice

Make it a habit.

The simplest way to sharpen all of this is the JotBot Daily: one shared word a day, the same for everyone, no friend required. It's a low-stakes rep for your deduction. A few quiet minutes, a streak to keep, a result to share.
